Why I blog?

  1. The purpose of creating this blog was to share information about how to apply for the Canadien Student visa and the college in Vancouver as well as some English test preparing tips. Because I found that there weren't many useful posts on the internet while I needed, most of them were articles written by agencies. Therefore, I wanted to blog down my journey of study abroad, as a demonstration to those who have the same desire to study here.
  2. I'm convinced that the best way for me to learn is by doing. Although I already enrolled as a digital marketing student, there would be plenty of chances that allow me to learn and practice. Yet, I know there's not enough, so I built this website to gain more hands-on experiences in terms of web design, content writing, SEO, and much more that would make me grow better and faster.

What challenges have I encountered so far?

  1. Lacking free effective keywords search tools, and most of them don't offer relevant data based on my native language.
  2. Having difficulties in sharing things based on my personal experience due to COVID 19, because it's a micro-niche blog, and it becomes harder for me to write posts like my school life, or things need to know to live in Vancouver with being quarantined and having virtual-learning at home.

What do I do to deal with the current problems?

Instead of waiting for the world works the best for me, I decided to sharpen my skills and obtain knowledge to adapt to the aforementioned restrictions. I tried to practice more of SERPs analysis, site audit, and link building for SEO. Despite putting all that effort might not let me be the top1 overnight, but I would keep working on my disadvantages and enhance my advantages because I know it would be a long way to go to be a qualified marketer.
