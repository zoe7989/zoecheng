Why KDC?

Due to my passion for dancing, I decided to offer a kind of service for people who like music and also want to learn street dance, with the friends I know in the dance club at university.

What I did I do with it?

Because I was interested in online marketing, so I was in charge of everything regarding this idea. At first, I was excited and did everything I know to execute it from finding good photography, building a Facebook fan page, creating and editing videoes and posts, sharing to relevant FB clubs, to learn how to advertise on Facebook.

In about two weeks of efforts, our FB page received around 400 followers without running FB ads, and our busiest time was 6 classes per week.

What kind of challenges I'd faced?

However, it wasn't easy to manage this small business, there are some reasons for the difficulties. Firstly, most teachers (my friends) in KDC all had a full-time job, which caused uncertainty to match the right schedule for customers, but also hard to accept the orders when we could. Secondly, lacking our own website would be a factor that we couldn't be able to drive more organic traffic, therefore losing more opportunities to build brand awareness and create conversions. Last but not least, there were not enough trustworthy teachers to cope with unexpected course-related issues such as failed to find a class location and customers' complain, etc.

How to change?

Surprisingly, although I've stopped promoting KDC, there are still parents requesting information in terms of our service. After a throughout exam of KDC, If I would restart the business someday, building a website and recruiting qualified teachers will definitely be the first and foremost priority.
